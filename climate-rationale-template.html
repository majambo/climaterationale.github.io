Building Climate Rationale

Developing robust climate rationales is essential for effective climate action planning, policy development, and project funding.

A strong climate rationale links observed and projected climate changes to specific impacts on communities, ecosystems, and infrastructure. It provides the scientific foundation for adaptation and mitigation efforts.

Climate Rationale Framework

A systematic approach to developing evidence-based climate rationales

Begin by collecting and analyzing relevant climate data:

  • Historical climate trends (temperature, precipitation, extreme events)
  • Future climate projections under different scenarios
  • Regional climate models and downscaled data
  • Uncertainty analysis and confidence levels
View Data Analysis Guide

Identify and evaluate climate change impacts on:

  • Natural systems (ecosystems, biodiversity, water resources)
  • Human systems (agriculture, health, infrastructure)
  • Economic sectors and livelihoods
  • Vulnerable populations and communities
Download Impact Assessment Templates

Assess vulnerability by analyzing:

  • Exposure to climate hazards
  • Sensitivity of systems to climate impacts
  • Adaptive capacity of communities and institutions
  • Existing resilience factors and gaps
View Vulnerability Assessment Framework

Design appropriate response strategies based on the evidence:

  • Adaptation measures to address identified vulnerabilities
  • Mitigation actions to reduce emissions and enhance sinks
  • Integrated approaches that address multiple objectives
  • Prioritization based on feasibility, cost-effectiveness, and co-benefits
Explore Strategy Development Tools

Effectively document and communicate the climate rationale:

  • Compile evidence in a structured format
  • Develop clear narratives linking climate change to impacts and responses
  • Tailor communication for different stakeholders and purposes
  • Create visual representations of key findings and recommendations
