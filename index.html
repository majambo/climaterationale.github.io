Climate Rationale

A climate rationale is a scientific explanation of how a proposed project will address climate change. It's used to help policymakers, donors, and service providers understand the climate risks and vulnerabilities of a project.

Purpose

  • To help ensure that projects address climate change
  • To help ensure that projects contribute to climate resilience
  • To help ensure that projects are based on the best available climate science

Objectives

  • Understand the Role of Climate Data in Project Development – Learn how to use scientific data to justify climate-related interventions.
  • Identify Historical Climate Trends – Analyze past climate patterns using real datasets.
  • Project Future Climate Risks – Utilize climate models to assess potential impacts.
  • Quantify Climate Vulnerabilities – Apply climate indices and risk assessment tools to evaluate affected populations and ecosystems.
  • Strengthen Decision-Making in Climate Proposals – Learn how to integrate climate data into funding applications to enhance project credibility.

Expected Outcomes

  • Analyze and Interpret Climate Data – Use historical trends and projections to inform project design.
  • Develop a Climate Rationale – Draft a compelling justification for a project proposal, demonstrating the climate connection.

  • Defining Climate Rationale

    What?

    Climate rationale refers to the justification for how a proposed project directly addresses climate change. It is based on scientific evidence, climate data, and risk assessments to demonstrate the need for intervention. A strong climate rationale links project objectives with observed climate trends, future projections, and vulnerability assessments.

    Why?
    • Ensures that projects are designed to specifically address climate-related challenges rather than general development issues.
    • Helps secure funding from climate finance mechanisms (e.g., Green Climate Fund, Adaptation Fund) by demonstrating clear climate impact.
    • Aligns with national climate policies and international frameworks like the Paris Agreement and Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
    • Supports evidence-based decision-making for climate adaptation and mitigation actions.

Useful Platforms for Climate Data

While most countries recognise the importance of adaptation to climate change, there is limited access to reliable information on climate impacts and risks which should inform the selection of adaptation strategies. This is also true for the operationalisation of adaptation goals which are defined in Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) and National Adaptation Plans (NAPs): How to translate adaptation goals into concrete actions? Hence, there is a need for science-based adaptation planning which requires sound climate risk analyses and assessment of potential adaptation strategies. To address this issue, there are a number of platforms that provide comprehensive climate risk analyses as listed below. Explore the platforms.

  • Africa Adaptation Atlas - Provides spatially explicit climate risk assessments to support decision-making in Africa.
  • Climate Change Knowledge Portal - Offers global, regional, and country-level climate data, risk assessments, and projections.
  • Climate Risk Analyses for Adaptation Planning in Sub-Saharan Africa - Delivers detailed climate risk and impact analyses to aid adaptation strategies in Africa.
